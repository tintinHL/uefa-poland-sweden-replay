Petition: Replay Sweden vs Poland (3–2) — World Cup Qualifier

Started by Tomasz Wiśniewski · 31 March 2026 Urgent

Following yesterday's World Cup Qualifier between Sweden and Poland (3–2), we — the undersigned — demand that FIFA immediately order a full replay of the match under neutral officiating.

The match, played on 31 March 2026, ended in a controversial 3–2 victory for Sweden, with Viktor Gyökeres scoring a decisive 88th-minute winner. However, the match was marred by several critical refereeing decisions that materially affected the outcome:

  • A clear penalty denied to Poland in the 72nd minute after a blatant handball in the box by Lagerbielke, confirmed by multiple camera angles — the same player who had scored Sweden's second goal
  • Viktor Gyökeres' 88th-minute winner should have been disallowed — replays show a clear push on the Polish centre-back in the build-up, which VAR inexplicably chose not to review
  • Zalewski's equaliser in the 33rd minute was initially flagged offside before being correctly overturned, yet the same scrutiny was not applied to Sweden's goals
  • A second yellow card not given to Elanga for a cynical tactical foul on Zieliński in the 82nd minute, just 6 minutes before the winning goal

Poland fought back from 2–0 down through Zalewski (33') and Świderski (55') to level the match at 2–2, only to be denied by a combination of poor officiating and inconsistent VAR application in the closing minutes.

📢 UPDATE (1 April 2026): The petition has gained significant traction overnight. Multiple sports journalists have shared the campaign. UEFA's press office has acknowledged receipt of the formal complaint.

We call upon FIFA's Disciplinary Committee to invoke Article 77 of the FIFA Disciplinary Code, which provides grounds for annulment and replay in cases where match-altering officiating failures can be demonstrated.

Every signature counts. Share this petition widely to ensure the integrity of European football is upheld. Our voices will be heard.
