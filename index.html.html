Thousands of supporters and football integrity advocates have called on the UEFA Match Review Board to order a full replay of last night's World Cup 2026 qualifying fixture between Poland and Sweden, following a series of deeply controversial officiating decisions that many believe fundamentally altered the course of the match.

⚠ Official Grounds for Review The petition cites three disallowed Polish goals in the first half, a disputed penalty award in the 74th minute, and the failure of the VAR system to review a potential red-card offence by a Swedish defender. Supporters claim the cumulative effect of these decisions denied Poland a fair result.

The petition, which has already gathered over 156,000 signatures in less than 12 hours, is being led by the Polish Supporters' Coalition in coordination with several football integrity organisations across Europe. It calls for an independent review of all officiating decisions and, if irregularities are confirmed, a full replay at a neutral venue.

UEFA's Match Review Board has acknowledged receipt of a formal complaint from the Polish Football Association (PZPN) and has stated it will consider any petition that surpasses the required threshold of 200,000 verified signatures from across UEFA member nations.

Supporters from Sweden, Norway, Germany and France have also added their signatures, citing concern for the integrity of the qualification process ahead of the 2026 World Cup.
